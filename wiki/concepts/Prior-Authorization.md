# Prior Authorization

**Type:** Healthcare Process
**Domain:** Health Insurance Operations

## Summary

Prior authorization is a utilization management process used by health plans to determine whether a prescribed medication, treatment, or medical service is medically necessary and covered under a patient's insurance plan before the service is provided. This process involves clinical documentation review, often by nurses or medical directors, to ensure appropriateness and compliance with clinical guidelines.

## Process Components

**Documentation Intake:**
- Fax submissions
- PDF uploads
- Provider portal submissions
- Direct provider uploads
- Electronic health record integrations

**Review Workflow:**
- Initial screening for completeness
- Clinical documentation review
- Guidelines verification
- Medical director consultation (if needed)
- Approval/determination communication

**Key Stakeholders:**
- Requesting providers
- Reviewing nurses/clinical staff
- Medical directors
- Patients/members
- Health plan administrators

## Performance Metrics

**Common Metrics:**
- Turnaround time (industry average varies)
- Rework rate (due to missing information)
- First-pass approval rate
- Provider satisfaction scores
- Regulatory compliance metrics

**Case Study Metrics:**
- 4.2-day average turnaround time
- 22% rework rate due to missing information
- Frequent provider status inquiry calls

## Challenges

**Operational Challenges:**
- Manual review processes
- Multi-channel documentation complexity
- Missing or incomplete documentation
- Provider workflow friction
- Regulatory compliance burden
- Volume scalability

**Quality Considerations:**
- Clinical appropriateness assessment
- Consistency in application of guidelines
- Provider education and communication
- Patient access to care
